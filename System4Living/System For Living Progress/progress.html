System For Living Progress









On our first day back to university we were briefed on “A System For Living” and were assigned into teams of five to build momentum for projects, which later we would take on by ourselves. A strong focus of the brief is to look at combatting environmental issues in modern life. While brain storming our team came up with multiple ideas around the theme of waste. How to prevent it, reduce it or make profit from it. For example in the UK the average person generates 4.3 pounds of waste per day. This is 1.6 pounds more then most people produced in 1960. Approximately 55% of the 220 million tons of waste generated each year ends up in landfill.

Your average landfill produces 2,000 litres of methane a day. That is the equivalent to what the ¼ of the population of the worlds of cars produce daily. This methane is wasted energy being polluted into our climate. This amount of gas could fuel every car in Belgium for 2 weeks. For this project my team are looking to see if there is any possibility to enhance this commodity.

The idea is to produce a bin for every property in the U.K. Each bin condenses each households waste into renewable energy with the added incentive of money to encourage recycling. Each bin is accompanied by an app, which allows each consumer to keep a record of their “Chute.” This is a privatised scheme not run by the government. Each bin would cost £1,000 with an estimated return within 5 months of purchase. “Chute” the company extracts the fuel and pays ¼ of the profit to the household.

The biggest benefit of this product is that instead of valuable gasses escaping into our atmosphere, we will enhance the gas and use it to fuel the society we live in. Chute is for profit, but for the interest of the world.

Cookie was my answer to helping fight against food waste. 38% of all food is wasted and not eaten after being cooked. This could potentially be that people are cooking too much food or restaurants are throwing away edible products. Not even mentioning the food wasted before prepared which is mentioned within the research part of this page 38% is still a huge amount. This app was connected to the webpage waste above as I used it to see what the problems are, and from that work out how we can fix them. This app combatted the key suggestions, which can help prevent the waste of food.

I wanted to link a community aspect within the app, using API systems like on apps like Tinder the app would geographically connect people through food. After Cookie came Cookiebook, I did try and come up with a better name and a new logo, however through my process Cookiebook felt natural. It was a play on the phrase “Cooking Book.” At the time I liked the logo and colour scheme a lot. Cookiebook is a different way of combatting food waste: We all have food in our cupboards and fridge which a perishable, we try and use them but sometimes they go to waste. Instead of letting these go to waste, Cookiebook encourages people to use the dregs. For example, I might get home and all I have in the fridge is some broccoli, which is about to go off tomorrow, cream which is running out of date and some fish. I might have no idea how to combine these together to make a delicious meal. Instead of calling a takeaway Cookiebook allows its users to type in these ingredients and suggests recipes, which can turn these scraps into a dish of beauty. This is combatting food waste on a personal level.

Rating System



Initially I was going to put multiple mushrooms (logo) to symbolise the rating of each recipe, like a 5 star rating system. Instead I created a system where each level of cooking ability determined by how other users rate your food. This will save space and will allow me to eventually expand the levels you can get to. Like all "Freemium" Games, the idea is to trigger the users addictive personality by rewarding them with digital prizes. In this case, you can only submit a recipe if you have rated 25 recipes. Once you reach the first level you can then submit 5 recipes, once you submit 5 you go up to the next level. You cannot submit anymore recipes or get to the next level unless you have an average of 70% or over of positive ratings. However you can buy recipe entries for £1.







Marketing Plan



People who would use Forage inherently like food. To find an audience of people that like food where better to look then restaurants?
